<h2 class="section-heading" id="about">About</h2>

I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at the [Broad Institute](https://www.broadinstitute.org/). My research focuses on developing and applying statistical methods to elucidate the genetic architecture of complex traits, with a particular emphasis on cardiometabolic and plasma lipid-related outcomes. I conduct large-scale GWAS meta-analyses and rare-variant association tests to identify both common and rare genetic variations that contribute to disease risk. Methodologically, I am interested in developing Bayesian frameworks to quantify the replicability of pleiotropic loci across studies and populations. I am an active member of the [NHLBI Trans-Omics for Precision Medicine (TOPMed) Program](https://topmed.nhlbi.nih.gov) and the [Global Lipids Genetics Consortium (GLGC)](https://www.lipidgenetics.org), collaborating closely with [Dr. Pradeep Natarajan](https://natarajanlab.mgh.harvard.edu/pradeep-natarajan/), Dr. Jacqueline Dron, [Dr. Xihong Lin](https://hsph.harvard.edu/profile/xihong-lin/), and [Dr. Xihao Li](https://www.xihaoli.org).

I received my PhD in Biostatistics from [Boston University](https://www.bu.edu/), advised by [Dr. Ching-Ti Liu](https://www.bu.edu/sph/profile/ching-ti-liu/) and [Dr. Gina Peloso](https://www.bu.edu/sph/profile/gina-peloso/). Prior to that, I earned my master's degree in Biostatistics from [Yale University](https://ysph.yale.edu/), where I was advised by [Dr. Denise Esserman](https://ysph.yale.edu/profile/denise_esserman/) and [Dr. Hongyu Zhao](https://ysph.yale.edu/profile/hongyu_zhao/). 

I completed my undergraduate studies in Applied Biology with Biotechnology at [The Hong Kong Polytechnic University](https://www.polyu.edu.hk/), graduating with First Class Honours.
